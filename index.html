Osmo Product FAQ

An electrolyte is a mineral that dissolves in water and splits into electrically charged ions — sodium (Na⁺), potassium (K⁺), magnesium (Mg²⁺), calcium (Ca²⁺). Every nerve firing, muscle contraction, and cognitive function is an electrochemical event powered by these ions.

Plain water has zero electrical charge. Electrolytes are the operating system. Water is just the medium they work in. Sodium activates SGLT1 co-transport, pulling water actively into the bloodstream up to 3× faster than plain water.

Four functions: fluid regulation (water follows electrolytes via osmosis), muscle contraction/relaxation (calcium triggers, magnesium releases), nerve signal transmission (sodium fires, potassium resets — millions of times per second), energy metabolism (magnesium powers ATP synthase).

At 1% dehydration, concentration and short-term memory are measurably impaired. At 2%, reaction time slows 15%. At 5%, cognitive performance degrades like 0.08% blood alcohol.

Sodium (400mg) — master electrolyte, drives SGLT1 fluid absorption. Potassium (250mg) — balances sodium, intracellular hydration. Magnesium (100mg citrate) — 300+ enzymatic reactions, muscle relaxation, ~70–80% urban Indian adults deficient. Taurine (1,300mg) — Osmo's differentiator: the body's primary organic osmolyte, cellular hydration no other India brand has. Boron — reduces urinary Mg/Ca loss by 30%.

Loss is continuous and invisible. Sweat (700–1,000mg sodium per litre in Indian heat), ambient heat (passive loss at 35°C+ without exercise — sitting in Delhi in May causes depletion), caffeine (3 coffees/day drains Na and Mg continuously — the coffee crash is partly an electrolyte event), alcohol (hangover is almost entirely electrolyte + B-vitamin depletion), chronic stress (cortisol directly increases urinary Mg excretion).

Common signs: night muscle cramps (Mg depleted — Mg supplementation reduces cramp frequency ~50%), 3pm energy crash (Na + B-vitamins depleted by lunch), poor sleep/restless legs (Mg depleted — Mg improves sleep quality ~17%), frequent illness (zinc depleted — doubles cold recovery time), dizziness when standing (low Na, low blood volume), afternoon headache despite drinking water (cellular dehydration — most underdiagnosed hydration problem).
