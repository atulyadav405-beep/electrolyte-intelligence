Sweetened with monk fruit and stevia, it is explicitly targeting urban professionals, students, and commuters — not just athletes — using the tagline 'Water is not enough.' Liquid I.V. India via HUL: Global Giant Enters With Mass Distribution Muscle and Premium Positioning

Hindustan Unilever (HUL) has brought Liquid I.V. — one of the world's largest electrolyte brands by revenue — into the Indian market. HUL's distribution infrastructure (7M+ retail touchpoints, Flipkart/Amazon priority placement, and Modern Trade dominance) gives Liquid I.V. an immediate nationwide reach that no D2C-native brand can match. Liquid I.V. uses its proprietary Cellular Transport Technology (CTT) messaging and a multiplier hydration claim ('2–3x faster hydration than water alone') which resonates strongly with wellness-conscious urban consumers. Its global brand equity and Instagram-native branding make it a credible premium import.

White space: Liquid I.V.'s CTT and hydration-multiplier claims are its entire scientific narrative — but its formulation and dosing are built for Western sweat profiles and are not India-climate-adapted. Osmo's opening is to publish a direct, data-led comparison on sweat sodium concentration in Indian heat vs. Western benchmarks, demonstrating why Indian bodies in 40°C+ humidity need a fundamentally different electrolyte ratio. Additionally, Osmo can drive a 'Made by Indians, for Indian bodies, tested in Indian conditions' campaign, leveraging local sports medicine partnerships to establish sovereign scientific credibility that an imported brand cannot authentically claim.
