What's happening

Science Credibility Is the New Moat as Clean-Label Scrutiny Intensifies Globally
HIGH
What's happening
A measurable global shift is underway where consumers are demanding not just clean ingredient lists but verifiable science behind electrolyte ratios, osmolality claims, and absorption mechanisms. This follows a wave of high-profile third-party lab investigations in the US and UK exposing mislabelled sodium content and unsubstantiated absorption claims from mid-tier brands. Organizations like Informed Sport and NSF Certified for Sport are seeing record certification applications in 2025-26. Simultaneously, sports dietitians and physician-influencers on platforms like X and YouTube have become primary trust arbiters for premium hydration decisions, especially among the 28-42 urban professional cohort.
Marketing angle
Osmo's entire brand thesis is built on science credibility, making this global trend a structural tailwind rather than a threat — but only if it moves aggressively to make that credibility visible and verifiable. Concrete actions include publishing osmolality and electrolyte ratio data transparently on packaging and the D2C site, pursuing or prominently displaying any third-party certifications, and activating partnerships with Indian sports medicine professionals and RDs who carry authority with the target audience. With May being both peak hydration season and a period of heightened health advisory communication from Indian government bodies due to heatwaves, earned media through credible health voices will have outsized reach and conversion impact right now.
