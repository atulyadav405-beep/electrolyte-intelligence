Peak Heatwave Corridor Hits 47°C Across Rajasthan–UP–Bihar Belt
critical
What's happening
The Indo-Gangetic Plain is recording its most intense May heatwave since 2022, with Rajasthan, UP, and Bihar districts breaching 46–47°C and the IMD issuing red alerts for 11 states. Urban heat island effects in Delhi, Lucknow, and Patna are driving daytime temperatures 3–4°C above regional averages, extending heat stress well into evening hours for the first time this season.
Why it matters
At core temperatures above 38°C, sweat rates in unacclimatized urban adults can exceed 1.5 litres per hour, depleting sodium, potassium, and magnesium faster than plain water can replace, creating acute electrolyte deficits that manifest as cramps, fatigue, and heat exhaustion.
