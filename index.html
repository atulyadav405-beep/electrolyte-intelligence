India heatwave 2026 — record early onset, 98/100 hottest cities
Critical
What's happening
India is in the grip of an extreme early-onset heatwave in 2026, with 98 of the world's 100 hottest cities within its borders. Temperatures have surged past 45°C across northern, central, and eastern India. Akola, Maharashtra recorded 46.9°C on April 27. Power demand hit a record 256 GW — a level usually seen in June–July. IMD forecasts above-normal heatwave days through June across east, central, and northwest India.
Why it matters for electrolytes
Extreme heat accelerates electrolyte loss through sweat. A 2026 study confirmed that even a single day of heatwave increases daily mortality by a measurable percentage. Long-term heat exposure increases stroke risk by 18% in Indians over 45, with a 40% jump for those with diabetes or hypertension. The body is losing far more than water — this is the core science message.

"This isn't normal summer heat. Your body is losing more than water." Lead with empathy and science. Position Osmo as the evolved upgrade from ORS, which the government is actively pushing. Avoid promotional tone — awareness-first.
